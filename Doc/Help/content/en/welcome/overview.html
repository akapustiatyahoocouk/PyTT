PyTT overview

PyTT (Python Time Tracker) is a tool that allows a computer user to record their daily progress through various activities they have to undertake.

PyTT was originally designed to meet the needs of software engineers and developers, who have to perform many different tasks during their working hours, switching from one to another; however, it can be useful for e.g. office workers - or, indeed, anyone who needs to do many things at once and to keep track of how their time is used.

The key features of PyTT are:

  • Ease of use.
  • Portability.
  • Localisation.

Ease of use

Before PyTT was born, a prototype (TimeTracker) was created and used in real-life projects, both commercial and open source, for several years. That gave a unique perspective to what was done right in the first place, what featurtes needed to be added, and what features needed to change to better suit the needs of a PyTT user.

Portability

PyTT is written in Python. This means that it can be used on any platform that supports Python 3.8 or later - this includes Windows (7/10/11), Linux (GNOME and KDE UIs), and MacOS. PyTT has, in fact, been successfully tested on all these platforms.

Localisation

PyTT is designed from the ground up to be usable internationally. It will "speak" the language of your choice; additional language packs can be added to a PyTT installation as needed.
